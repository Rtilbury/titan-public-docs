# Titan-X Regulatory Positioning

The regulatory landscape for AI is entering a new phase defined by transparency, privacy, and explainability. Titan-X was designed specifically for this environment.

This document summarises how Titan-X fits into the upcoming EU, UK, and global frameworks.

---

## The 2026 Shift

New AI regulations will impose strict rules on:

- Profiling  
- Behavioural inference  
- Automated decision-making  
- High-risk AI systems  
- Cross-border data movement  
- Data-retention windows  
- Auditability & explainability  

Titan-X is engineered to sit **outside** the highest-risk categories.

---

## Why Titan-X Is Low-Risk Under New Regulation

### **1. No Personal Data**
The majority of high-risk obligations relate to personal data, identity, profiling, or inference.  
Titan-X does not use any of these.

### **2. No Profiling or Behavioural Histories**
Titan-X processes live signals only.  
It does not create ongoing behavioural models.

### **3. Explainability Built-In**
Outputs are transparent and rule-based.  
This places Titan-X in the “minimal-risk” category.

### **4. Customer-Controlled Deployment**
Data never leaves the customer’s environment.  
This eliminates cross-border risk and reduces DPIA scope.

### **5. No Automated Decisions With Legal Effects**
Titan-X provides **adaptive experience recommendations**, not automated legal/economic decisions.
